Digital Painting

Drawing digitally can be kind of disorienting for me. My brain simply makes better sense of things when I'm holding a paintbrush and making a mess of my clothes with paint. I'm sure I'll make sense of it someday.

Even though I'm not overly thrilled about the way this piece came out, I still get really happy looking at it. I enjoyed the process. That's what it's all about, at the end of the day.

Podracer Engine

Foley

A foley project where I attempted to recreate the sound of Ani's podracer engine as seen in Star Wars Episode I: The Phantom Menace.

To create the sound, I recorded and edited sounds I happened upon that I thought would work well. This included my school's power generator, a double decker bus, the kind of push handle on a door that makes a loud clank sound, and a very noisy cricket.

EmotiQuench Commercial

Video

In a tech class I took, we were tasked to create an imaginary product, and then develop an ad to pitch to the class.

Script

INT - Home/Void - Midday

Present self is in the void.

Present

Hey you! You look sad, disheartened, and or miserable. Ever wish that could change in an instant?

Cut to past self in house.

Past

Yeah.

Present self whips out bottle with liquid inside.

Present

Well, now you- wait a minute. What are you doing in this ad?

Past

You mean what am I doing in this ad?

Present

Yes, that’s. What I said.

Past

Why don’t you tell me? This is your ad. Or. Our ad.

Present

Oh, yeah, that’s… weird.

Past

I’m curious to know what this ad even is. Care to explain, other me?

Past self notices the bottle Present self is holding.

Past

Hey, what’s that?

Present

It’s the product I’m advertising.

Past

Oh, ok. Continue then.

Present

Thanks. *brief pause* Well, now you can, with EmotiQuench! With an assortment of flavours available, you can make any of your insecurities disappear.

Past

How does it work?

Present

Simple. Our scientists have discovered a way to create artificial emotions that last up to twelve hours. All you need to do is drink and enjoy.

Past

Do you have a cherry flavour?

Present

You don’t even like cherry.

Past

I know.

Present

Uh, we do actually. Cherry Cheer, Lemonade Luck, Cola Confidence, and Pineapple Productivity are our flavours-

Past

Three of those aren’t even emotions.

Present

Oh, I guess so. We use the term emotion a little loosely. They still work, though.

Past

I would be more specific if I were you.

Present

You are me, and you weren’t.

Past and Present laugh for a moment.

Past

I guess you’re right. So you’re telling me that if I need to work on a group project with a bunch of strangers, all I need is a little Cola Confidence and I won’t be painfully awkward?

Present nods.

Past

Wow. I can’t wait to invent that so I can have some.

Present

I woke up one morning only to remember I had an essay I hadn’t even started due in an hour. All I needed was a little Pineapple Productivity, and I got an easy 95.

Past

Hey, what’s that?

Present

This is our newest flavour, Dragonfruit Depression-

Past

No, behind you!

An anomaly starts whisking Present away.

Present

Woah! Remember to buy EmotiQuench for a worthwhile lifeeeee~

Past is silent for a moment, shocked.

Past

I hate that guy.

END OF SCENE (OSCARS FOR EVERYONE)

I knew I wanted to take a more comedic approach, and drew inspiration for my script from skits done by YouTuber Drew Gooden. I then shot half the ad in front of a greenscreen, and the other half in the comfort of my own home. When editing, I thought it may add to the comedic tone I wanted if I had a background with an obvious watermark.

When presenting our ads, we also had to create pitch slides to present in addition to our ad. Unfortunately, my pitch deck has been lost to the cruel fate of being deleted alongside my public school email. Tragic.

For what it's worth, despite the numerous quality pitches with genuine product ideas my classmates brought, it seemed my silly approach was appreciated by my peers as I was voted to win second place after the pitches. I recieved a prize of three toy Paw Patrol cars my teacher found at Mastermind Toys. I still have them. For whatever reason.
